Hello! My name is Thomas. I am a human with a passion for technology and design currently based in Chicago, IL. I am currently a Senior Software Development Engineer at 4C Insights. I have a B.S. in Computer Science from Loyola University Chicago.

As an Engineer at 4C, I mainly work on a SaaS ads management tool for ad activation, planning and measuring in premium marketplaces. Currently I am helping to spearhead an effort to transition our entire frontend codebase to React from Ember.js by creating a solid reusable component library for cross-platform usage. Lately I have spent time pushing foward our CI/CD efforts and working to get our application in a highly available Kubernetes cluster. I am part of a small team that planned and built (and continues to do so!) a cross-publisher audience creation and management tool. Users can target, build, upload and expansion model audiences based on different inputs to use cross-channel. I work with multiple third-party API and data integrations as well as with 4C's own in-house data for audience building, data manipulation and publishing. Additionally, I have had the opportunity to help lead and maintain the building and design of a high-level data visualization tool that makes use of TV and social data to help brands better understand their impact and gain audience insights in addition to how they compare to their competitors. I built a chat-ops bot to help the team monitor the status of our continuous integration tests and manage our continous deployments and tools pipeline. I also mentor junior engineers and often help evaluate engineering candidates.

In the processing of building and working at 4C, I've gained experience working with a full stack of languages such as JavaScript (ES6), Python, HTML, CSS/LESS/SCSS, Node.js and technologies such as React, Ember.js, Flask, MySQL, MongoDB, Redis, Redux, Immutable.js, AWS, SQLAlchemy and Celery.

I spend much of my free time researching and exploring the confluence of technology, design and climate change/human rights. And decentralized technologies (very excited about IPFS). I love to run, cook/bake, travel and read (currently Homo Deus: A Brief History of Tomorrow). I also enjoy photography, cartography, biking and hiking.
